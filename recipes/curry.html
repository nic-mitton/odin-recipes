Japanese Chicken Curry

Delicious Japanese chicken curry recipe for a weeknight dinner!

Tender pieces of chicken, carrots, and potatoes cooked in a rich savory curry sauce, this Japanese version of curry is a must-have for your family meal.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs boneless, skinless chicken thighs
  • 2 yellow onions
  • 2 carrots
  • 3 Yukon gold potatoes
  • 1 tsp ginger (grated)
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 1 Fuji apple
  • 1½ tbsp neutral oil (for cooking)
  • salt (to taste)
  • pepper (to taste)

For the Curry Sauce

  • 7 oz. Japanese Curry Roux (often sold in 3.5oz boxes)
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp ketchup

For Serving

  • 8 servings cooked Japanese short-grain rice

Steps

Prep

  1. Slice onion into wedges.
  2. Peel carrots and cut rangiri style.
  3. Peel potatoes and cut into cubes. Soak potatoes in water for 15 minutes to remove excess starch.
  4. Peel and grate Ginger. Mince Garlic
  5. Peel and core apple. Grate the apple and set aside.
  6. Trim fat from chicken thighs. Cut into bite-size pieces and season to taste with salt and pepper.

Cook the Curry

  1. Heat 1½ tbsp neutral oil in a large pot over medium heat and add onions.
  2. Sauté onions, stirring occasionally, until translucent and tender. Roughly 5 minutes.
  3. Add Garlic and Ginger
  4. Add Chicken, cook until outside is no longer pink.
  5. Add carrots and stir to mix.
  6. Add 4 cups water.
  7. Heat until nearly boiling. Skim scum and foam from surface. Repeat skimming as needed over next few steps.
  8. Add grated apple, honey, and a pinch of salt.
  9. Broth should be barely covering ingredients. Adjust liquid if necessary.
  10. Allow to simmer uncovered for 15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  11. Add potatoes. Allow to simmer until potatoes are tender, about 15 minutes.
  12. Turn off the heat. Separate curry roux into individual pieces and place in a medium mixing bowl
  13. Use a ladle to pour broth over curry roux. Try to avoid including meat/vegetables with broth.
  14. Use a utensil (I recommend a potato masher) to dissolve curry roux into broth. Once fully dissolved, pour contents of mixing bowl back into pot.
  15. Add ketchup and soy sauce.
  16. Simmer about 5 minutes to allow curry to thicken, stirring often. If curry is too thick, water can be added to thin it.
  17. Serve curry with freshly steamed japanese short-grain rice.
